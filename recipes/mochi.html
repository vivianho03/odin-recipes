Mochi Waffle Recipe

A Japanese inspired dessert with a Western twist! Mochi Waffles can be eaten for breakfast or dessert.

Mochi Waffles can be topped with a multitude of toppings, ranging from fresh fruits, like strawberries and blueberries, to crunchy add ons such as nuts, wafers, and candy. It is often simply topped with condensed milk or powdered sugar since it is sweet as is, but some people like to add syrups, icecreams, and more.

The best mochi waffle is crunchy on the exterior of the waffle, and is sweet and chewy in the center when it is served immediately.

I personally like my desserts simple. When I eat mochi waffles, I simply add either add a light drizzle of condensed milk or some powdered sugar.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 1/4 cup mochiko flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Recipe instructions

  1. Preheat waffle iron.
  2. In a medium mixing bowl add milk, egg, and vanilla extract. Whisk until well combined. Set aside.
  3. In another medium mixing bowl add mochiko flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Whisk until well combined.
  4. Add wet ingredients to dry. Whisk until just combined.
  5. Spray nonstick cooking spray on the waffle iron. Add 1/4 to 1/2 a cup of the batter to the waffle iron. Cook for 3-5 minutes or until golden brown.
  6. Add toppings of your choice and ENJOY!

Notes

  • * This recipe is gluten free.
  • *Favorite toppings include: syrup, powdered sugar, fresh fruits, whipped cream, liliko'i butter.
  • *You can substitute dairy milk for your non-dairy milk of choice if you wish.
  • *You can add flavoring to the mochi waffle itself as well, like chocolate powder, matcha powder, and more!
